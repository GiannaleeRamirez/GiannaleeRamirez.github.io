• 1945-1960 South Korea becomes allies with the U.S to fight the korean war, South and North Korea split. After the war South Korea is continuing strong ties to the West and an ongoing U.S. military; so South Korea developed a robust/capitalist economy, and recently has made steps toward becoming a fully democratic nation.
  • 1962-1979 General Park Chung-hee in power. Martial law is imposed and then Park assassinated. General Chun Doo-hwan seizes power the following year.
  • 1987-1996 President Chun pushed out of office by student unrest so General Roh Tae-woo becomes President and grants greater political liberalisation and launches an anti-corruption drive. The First free parliamentary elections happen. Later South Korea joins the United Nations. 2 years later Kim Young Sam was elected.
  • 1997-2010 Kim Dae-jung becomes president and does the "sunshine policy" which offers unconditional economic and humanitarian aid to North Korea. In 2002 there was a battle between South Korean and North Korean naval vessels on their disputed sea border that left four South Koreans dead and 19 wounded. In 2007 South and North Korea agreed to restart high-level talks because of the North's nuclear tests. 2 years later South and North Korean warships exchange fire across a disputed sea border.
  • 2013-2020 South Korea accuses North of a cyber-attack. There were mass protests in Seoul against the government's economic policy and insistence on schools using state approved history books. In 2016 President Park Geun-Hye was impeached for allowing a personal friend with no government status get involved in government affairs. In 2018 Kim Jong-un becomes the first North Korean leader to enter the South when he meets South Korean President Moon Jae.
