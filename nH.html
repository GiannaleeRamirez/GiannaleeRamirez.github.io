• 1945-1960 North Korea's communist party is inaugurated and North Korea is now being backed up by the soviet union. The south declares independence which starts the north korean invasion and korean war. Armistice ended the korean war in 1953.
  • 1968-1994 In 1968 North Korea captured a US naval ship. In 1985 North Korea joined the international nuclear non-proliferation treaty. 1991 North Korea joins the United Nations. In !994 kim ii-sung died and his son succeeded as leader.
  • 1996-1998 Severe famine follows widespread floods and millions die of starvation.Later north korea sends thousands of troops to the demilitarised zone.
  • 2000-2003 North and South Korean naval vessels wage a gun battle in the Yellow Sea.In 2002 North Korea admits to having abducted 13 Japanese citizens in the 1970s and 1980s. Also North Korea announces it is reactivating nuclear facilities.
  • 2003-2007 North Korea test fires seven missiles, which crashes shortly after take-off despite it reportedly having the capability to hit the US.
  • 2007-2008 Kim Jong-il suffers a stroke
  • 2009-2010 North Korea scrapping all military and political deals with the South, and accused South Korea of "hostile intent".
  • 2013-2017 Kim Jong-un's uncle, Chang Song-thaek, is found guilty of attempting to overthrow the state and is executed. In 2014 two drones allegedly from North Korea were found in the south. North Korea confirms it has put its Yongbyon nuclear plant back into service and operation.
  • 2018-2020 Kim Jong-un becomes the first North Korean leader to enter the South when he meets South Korean President Moon Jae-in. A year later Kim Jong-un makes first visit to Russia, and receives support from President Vladimir Putin.
